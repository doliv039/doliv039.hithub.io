I'm Danielle Olivo and I'm from Hemet Ca. where I currently work full-time at my public Library as a page and adult literacy tutor. I recently graduated from my local community college, Mount San Jacinto College, where I studied English and then transferred to UCR to fulfill my bachelors degree in English. My plans after UCR are to enroll in a Masters Library Science program to fulfil my dream of becoming a librarian. .

I became interested in becoming a librarian when I started an internship for my city where I was hired to intern at my public library. I was taught by my well-educated experienced colleagues about the different technologies used for referencing books and checking them in for patrons. I also helped patrons who aren't familiar with using computers/windows/internet to navigate their way. As well as tutoring at the libraries adult literacy program which is very valuable to me because I'm able to help my community better themselves by teaching patrons involved with the literacy program to read. After my internship I was hired and made it a goal of mine to hold onto this opportunity and progress further.

Working at my public library and being a tutor for the past two years has given me valuable experiences where I'm still learning valuable information to this day and I'm able to help my community. My goal is to become more educated in this field to further my position where I currently work and to keep helping my community.
